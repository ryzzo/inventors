He changed the sewing industry in 1850 when he invented a sewing machine with a presser foot that could do 900 stitches per minute. Unlike prior machines, his gadget was able to sew continuously on any part of an object and could do so in curves, rather than straight rows. In 1857, he teamed up with Edward Clark, and together they launched what would become a very successful business: I.M. Singer & Company.
